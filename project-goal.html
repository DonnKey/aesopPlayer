The why

Most audio players available on the market are created for young people and their main function is playing music. They are small, have multiple features like shuffle, repeat, radio and even video playback.

All of these make them difficult or even impossible to operate by older, less tech savvy people often struggling with poor eyesight. These people often want to listen to audio books as an alternative to reading that becomes harder and harder as their sight deteriorates.

The goal

Homer Player is a project that aims at turning a regular Android tablet into a dedicated audio book player that can be easily used by visually impaired and elderly people.

The Homer Player application is created to run as the only application available to the user. This way all the other functions of the device don't become a distraction. It provides only the most basic features needed to play audio books.

With this application a multipurpose tablet can be converted into a simple to use, single purpose audio book player.

The assumptions

Homer Player is created with the following assumptions in mind:

  • focused on audio book playback, not music,
  • used at home,
  • controlled with imprecise gestures and subject to accidental touch,
  • the user can see but isn't able to make out letters or small UI controls,
  • the device serves only a single function (no access to other applications).
